The Apple II
General Information Fact Sheet

A general fact sheet / ReadMe regarding the technical specifications, production narrative and cultural context of the Apple II microcomputer. Information aggregated freely from open web platforms. Facts are designed to provide general primer for individuals not familiar with the machine.

Release Date: June 1977
Lead Developer: Steve Wozniac
Manufacturer: Apple Computer, Inc.
Original Price: $1,298 USD (approx. $5,476 2019)
Discontinued: May 1979

Operating System: Integer BASIC / Apple DOS
Central Processing Unit: MOS 6502 (1.023 MHz)
Memory: Ranged from 4KiB to 64KiB
Storage: Audio Cassette or Apple Disk II 5¼ in. Drive
Display RCA (NTSC Video Out)
Graphics: Low Resolution 16-color 40x48 / High Resolution 6-color 280x192
Sound: 1-Bit Built-In Audio Speaker

Peripheral Ports: 1-Bit Cassette Audio Jack Input / 1-Bit Cassette Audio Jack Output / Paddle Controllers / Parallel Port Card and Serial Port Card for Apple & Third Party Peripherals / SCSI

Predecessor: The Apple I (1976)
Successor: The Apple II Plus (1979)
